Take the first step!

If you’re interested in exploring career opportunities with Signature Flight Support, start by searching for a position that piques your interest. Try searching for current openings by position family, location, or keyword.

Help us get to know you

Ready to apply? Tell us about yourself by creating a profile and completing our online application. Take your time; we recommend setting aside at least 30 minutes so you can share more about who you are by highlighting your skills, experience, and knowledge. Have to run? No sweat, you can save your progress and finish later.

TIP: Store your user name and password where you can find them; you can use these credentials to log back into the system from time to time.

Our easy-to-use platform lets you choose from copying/pasting your resume, uploading a resume from your computer, or linking your Indeed or LinkedIn account!

We love that you’re interested in Signature Flight Support so we keep your application active for 60 days and consider you for future openings. See another role that interests you? After 60 days, just update your application and we will take another look; it’s like reconnecting with an old friend.

TIP: Be the first to know when new jobs are posted! Set up job alerts to notify you via email when we post new positions that match your criteria.

What to Expect

Now what?

Once you apply for a job, a member of our talent acquisition team reviews your qualifications and experience. If you’ve got the right stuff, a recruiter will contact you to learn more about your background and see if you’re a possible match for the role.

TIP: Our recruiters are happy to answer questions about our hiring process, timeline, and what it's like to work at Signature Flight Support so don’t be shy, ask away!

We believe the greatest compliment a company can receive is when you express an interest in sharing your time and talents as a team member. Unfortunately, due to the outstanding response we receive from candidates, we are unable to personally respond to each application; however, your interest in Signature Flight Support is genuinely appreciated.

Ring, Ring...

Congratulations! You’ve applied for a position and your skills fit the job. Way to go!

One of our Orlando, FL. based recruitment team members will contact you to schedule a phone interview. On the call, we may ask questions about your technical skills and proficiency, experience, and work history to determine if you are the best candidate for the job.

Show us your skills

For certain positions, we ask that all candidates complete an online assessment. Your recruiter will send you the link and we suggest allowing 30-45 minutes to complete the questionnaire.

TIP: Find a quiet place without distractions and complete the assessment from a desktop or laptop computer for the best experience (although you may use your tablet or phone.)

It's a pleasure to meet you!

You’re looking good! We liked your phone interview answers and you got great marks on your assessment. Now we invite you to get to know us in-person. Our on-site interviews can last from 30-60 minutes. We use this time to learn more about you and review core skills like: customer service, safety awareness, and your ability to apply what you learn in training. During your interview, you may meet with multiple managers who will ask questions related to your area of interest and how you’ve handled situations in the past. The interviewer may ask about your problem-solving skills and how you tend to respond in particular circumstances.

What happens next?

We know your time is valuable and we appreciate you taking time to meet with us. After your in-person interview, we will determine if you are the best candidate for the role. Adding new team members to the Signature family is a process we take very seriously so we like to make consensus-based decisions. Please be patient with us – your recruiter will keep in touch with you when feedback has been received and decisions made.

Do You Need Assistance?

If you need a reasonable accommodation for any part of the employment process, please send an email to recruiting@signatureflight.com and let us know the nature of your request and your contact information. Reasonable accommodations are considered on a case-by-case basis. Please note, we can only respond to inquiries concerning a request for reasonable accommodation via this e-mail address. On behalf of the entire Signature Flight Support family, we thank you for your interest in joining our team and wish you much success in your search for a rewarding and fulfilling career!

Know your SFS

Born from the merger of Page AvJet and Butler Aviation in 1992, supported by the addition of numerous independently owned Fixed Base Operators (FBO’s) over the past 25 years, and culminating with the acquisition of Landmark Aviation, Signature Flight Support (Signature) has grown to become the largest network of FBOs in the world.

  • 204 locations on five continents
  • Presence in 82% of top 50 business aviation markets in the United States
  • Over 6 million sq. ft. of hangar and office space
  • More than 5,000 employees around the world. In addition to a strategically constructed network of FBOs, Signature has ownership interests in a variety of aviation businesses including:
  • Signature TECHNICAir® MRO services at 18 locations in the US and UK
  • Gama Aviation Signature Aircraft Management operates a fleet of over 250 aircraft on behalf of their owners
  • Signature Select® extends Signature network benefits to independent FBOs
  • Signature ELITE Class offers commercial airline passengers private aviation style flight connection experiences

7 Signature Interview Tips

  1. Arrive early
    Show us you’re ready to work by being early to in-person interviews. Be sure to give yourself enough time to navigate to our location, park, and find the person you’re meeting with.
  2. Come with a Positive Attitude
    We all want to work with people we like and maintaining a positive attitude helps show us you're a team player.
  3. Do your homework
    Let us know you're interested in joining the Signature Flight Support Team by studying up on our core values and the services we offer.
  4. Tell us About Yourself
    Interviews are a way for you to learn more about Signature Flight Support and for us to learn more about you. We may ask you to tell us about your skills and experience and it's helpful to have thought about how you'll answer that question in advance.
  5. Be Confident
    Each potential new team member has a unique set of skills and experiences that they bring to the table. We value this diversity so don't be shy, tell us why you're a great fit for the role.
  6. Ask Questions
    Consider preparing some questions that you'd like to ask us about the future of Signature Flight and our culture.
  7. Follow Up
    After the interview, consider taking the initiative to follow up via email. Let us know your interest level in joining the team and share any thoughts or questions not covered during the meeting.

Which Role is Right for You?

If you enjoy:

  1. A fast-paced team environment
  2. Working outside with aircraft
  3. The freedom to think on your feet

Consider the following Signature roles:

  1. Line Service Technician (LST)
  2. Ground Support Equipment (GSE) Mechanic

If you enjoy:

  1. Greeting VIP guests
  2. An ever-changing exciting environment
  3. Working with a fun, fast-paced team

Consider the following Signature roles:

  1. Customer Service Representative
  2. Air Concierge

If you enjoy:

  1. Working independently and being part of a team
  2. Interacting with VIP customers
  3. Providing guidance and making decisions on the ramp
  4. Contributing to a team's culture and setting expectations for excellence

Consider applying for a role as an Operations Supervisor.

If you enjoy:

  1. Directing operational schedules
  2. Setting metrics for success
  3. Leading a hard-working team

Consider applying for a role as a Duty Manager.

If you enjoy:

  1. Creating a vision for the future
  2. Building relationships with team members and customers
  3. Developing leaders

Consider applying for a role as a General Manager.
